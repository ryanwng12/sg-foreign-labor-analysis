From 1.5 million workers in 1990 to over 4.1 million in 2025 — Singapore's workforce transformation has been driven by different forces in each sector. Construction became heavily dependent on foreign labour (82%), while Services — by far the largest sector — maintained a more balanced mix (~33%).

The COVID-19 shock of 2020 revealed just how deeply embedded foreign workers are in Singapore's economic structure: when ~173k left in a single year, it exposed structural dependence in essential sectors, not a surplus of replaceable workers.

Sources: MOM Administrative Records, MOM Labour Force Survey via SingStat, Dept of Statistics (GDP by industry). Non-resident employment is derived (total − resident). Pre-2009 data is reconstructed. See project README for methodology.
